Synthesizer Controller with Ferrofluid Visual

Overview

A 12-key mechanical keyboard controller for real-time audio synthesis, built around a custom KiCad PCB and Arduino Uno. Keystrokes map to synthesis and modulation parameters in Max/MSP, while a MOSFET-driven electromagnet animates ferrofluid in response to musical input.

Key Specs

  • PCB: 12-key mechanical keyboard, designed and laid out in KiCad
  • Microcontroller: Arduino Uno
  • Software: Max/MSP (real-time audio synthesis and modulation)
  • Actuator: MOSFET switching circuit driving a DC electromagnet
  • Enclosure: 3D printed in FDM, modeled in Fusion 360

What I Did

  • Designed and laid out a 12-key mechanical keyboard PCB in KiCad, then fabricated and assembled the board
  • Wrote Arduino firmware to read keyswitch states and communicate with Max/MSP over serial communication
  • Developed a Max/MSP patch mapping keystrokes to real-time synthesis parameters including pitch, modulation, and effects
  • Designed a MOSFET-based switching circuit to drive a DC electromagnet, creating dynamic ferrofluid motion synchronized to keyboard input
  • Modeled and 3D printed a custom electronic enclosure in Fusion 360 using FDM manufacturing
